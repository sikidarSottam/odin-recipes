Dal is a hearty and nutritious dish made from lentils simmered with aromatic spices and garnished with fresh herbs. This traditional recipe is both comforting and simple to prepare.

  • 1 cup lentils (red, yellow, or split pigeon peas)
  • 3 cups water
  • 1 medium onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon grated ginger
  • 1-2 green chilies, sliced (optional)
  • 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (optional)
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • Fresh coriander leaves for garnish

Steps :-

  1. Rinse the lentils thoroughly and add them to a pot with water. Bring to a boil, then simmer until the lentils are soft and fully cooked (about 20-25 minutes), skimming off any foam.
  2. In a separate pan, heat oil or ghee over medium heat. Add cumin and mustard seeds and cook until they begin to pop.
  3. Add the chopped onions, garlic, ginger, and green chilies. Saut√© until the onions turn golden brown.
  4. Stir in the chopped tomatoes, turmeric, and red chili powder. Cook until the tomatoes soften and form a thick paste.
  5. Pour the cooked lentils into the pan with the tomato-onion mixture. Mix well, add salt to taste, and let it simmer for an additional 5-10 minutes so that the flavors blend.
  6. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves and serve hot with rice or roti.
